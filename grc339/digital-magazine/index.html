Bryan Woo the goat

Bryan Woo wasn’t expected to shine, but four years after a modest college career and a sixth-round draft pick, he’s become the Mariners’ most valuable pitcher. Rising fast through the minors, refining his arsenal, and showing remarkable consistency, Woo is stepping up amid a shaky rotation. With a strong 2025 start and the spotlight on his unique skill set, can he help Seattle hold their division lead?

Best signing ever!

Jorge "Polo" Polanco, following a career low year in 2024, re-signed and has been a difference maker for the Mariners—providing clutch hits, solid defense at second base, and veteran consistency from both sides of the plate.

When it rains it dumps

Cal Raleigh has been the best catcher in baseball this season. He leads at the position in home runs and continues to call games like a veteran, guiding the Mariners’ pitching staff while providing power and leadership from behind the plate.

Walter’s weekly whoop-ass

Logan Gilbert has been lights out every week. Whether calling out standout performances or calling the shots on the latest games, Walter brings relentless passion and expertise that keeps fans informed and entertained.

Forever 51

On August 9, 2025, the Mariners will retire Ichiro’s No. 51 jersey to honor his Hall of Fame career. Ichiro set the MLB single-season hit record, won 10 Gold Gloves, and had over 4,300 combined hits. A Mariner legend and all-time great.

"Smile!" — Ken Griffey Jr., probably

Ken Griffey Jr. has made a successful transition to sports photography. Since retiring, he’s captured major events like the Masters Tournament, showing the same passion and talent behind the camera as he did on the field.

Muñoz still very good

Andrés Muñoz has been outstanding for the Mariners in 2025. In 20 appearances, he has a 0.00 ERA, 14 saves, and 27 strikeouts in 20.1 innings. Opponents hit just .171 against him, making him a key part of the bullpen’s success.

Kirby back soon

George Kirby is recovering from an injury and is expected to return soon. His comeback will bring much-needed depth and stability to the Mariners’ pitching rotation, giving the team a stronger chance as the season progresses.

Luis Castillo and Luis Castillo

The Mariners surprised fans by starting two different pitchers named Luis Castillo on consecutive days. This rare coincidence added an amusing twist to the rotation and sparked conversation about Luis Castillo 2.

Ben Williamson gets the call

Ben Williamson, the Mariners’ No. 13 prospect, was called up from Triple-A Tacoma in April 2025. He’s brought energy and outstanding defense to the infield, filling in while key players recover from injuries.

Jurrangelo Cijntje: switch pitcher

Jurrangelo Cijntje is a rare switch pitcher who can throw effectively with both arms. This unique ability allows him to adapt to batters and create matchup advantages. His versatility makes him a valuable asset on the mound.
